About The Project

Celeste is a critically acclaimed 2D platformer developed by Maddy Makes Games, released in 2018. It follows Madeline, a young woman climbing the mysterious Celeste Mountain while battling inner struggles like anxiety and self-doubt. The game features tight platforming mechanics, challenging yet fair difficulty, and an emotional narrative. Its pixel-art style, atmospheric soundtrack, and accessibility options make it a standout in the genre.
Here's why:
  • One of my favorite games since I first played it 4 years ago
  • It's both simple and complex. The mechanics are easy to understand but difficult to master, making it accessible while still rewarding skill.
  • Challenging to replicate. Despite its simple controls, Celeste has finely tuned physics, movement, and level design that make it tough to recreate.
  • Incredible art style
  • Emotional and meaningful story
  • Amazing soundtrack by Lena Raine
  • Masterful level design

My version

This section gives a clear and detailed overview of which parts of the original game I planned to make.

The minimum I will most certainly develop:

  • Collision with terrain. Ensuring the player interacts properly with the environment, including walls, floors, and platforms.
  • Basic player movement. Implementing walking, jumping, and gravity to make movement feel smooth and responsive.
  • Dash mechanic. The core feature of Celeste, allowing the player to dash in different directions.
  • Camera movement.
  • Basic level design. At least one playable level with terrain, obstacles, and hazards.
  • Death and respawn system
  • Wall climbing and wall jumping. Allowing the player to grab onto walls and jump off them.
  • Spikes
  • Basic UI. A simple interface displaying collected items and attempts.
  • Collectables. Strawberry's and dash refresh crystal.
  • Background music and sound effects

What I will probably make as well:

  • simple hair physics

What I plan to create if I have enough time left:

  • Particles and animations
  • A simple story implementation

How to play

Use this space to show useful examples of how a game can be played. Additional screenshots and demos work well in this space.

Controls

  • Left: Q
  • Right: D
  • Up: Z
  • Down: S
  • Jump: space
  • Grab: Right shift
  • Dash: L
This game is also fully playable with a basic game controller:
  • LSB = Left Stick: choose movement direction
  • RB = Right Bumper: Grab
  • RT = Right Trigger
  • A = A Button: jump
  • B = B Button: dash
